1. Shimla is located on seven major hills namely – Observatory Hill, Prospect Hill, Inveram Hill, Summer Hill, Elysium Hill, Jakhoo Hill and Bantony Hill.

2. The name “Shimla” is derived from the name of a Goddess – Shyamala Devi who is considered as an incarnation of another Hindu Goddess “Kali”

3. In a span of eight days and covering nearly 500 kilometers, The MTB Himalaya Mountain Biking Race is considered as the biggest mountain biking race in the South East Asia.

4. Founded by a Britisher in 1920, Shimla witnesses the only ice-skating rink in the entire South East Asia.

5. The best way to visit Shimla is via the “toy train” which runs on this track and to add more glory to this beautiful Rail tract, UNESCO declared it a World Heritage Site in 2008. This route comprises 864 bridges and 107 tunnels
